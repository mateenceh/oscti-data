Individuals identified with fluorescent ink: the explanations of the SPVM
30 November 2011, 5 pm

In events where there are mass arrests or when people are released under a condition, the investigators use, in some cases, the ink visible only to the UV rays (the same system as that used in some licensed institutions). In the case of the dismantling of buildings and facilities issued by the Borough of Ville-Marie for the Square Victoria last Friday, a dozen people, who were passive, were identified by investigators of the SPVM. Years ago, while traveling outside the US, I was out “clubbing” with some friends, one of whom was a police officer. He made a remark about the number of Americans in the area. I asked how he knew; he responded that one could spot them in the clubs as they passed under UV lighting. He was correct. Now almost every laundry product sold in the US has invisible fluorescent (semi permanent) DYES in them! They are marketed as “whiteners” or “brighteners”, and even as part of “fragrances.”

Savvey LEOs sometimes carry special flashlights/filters that can spot blood splatters — not that this “evidence” will be used in court — but to short cut some of the process involved in sorting out the “they said/I said” arguments about simple assaults or property crime. Anything to get the conversation started, so to speak.

Military uniforms are specially constructed and treated for different things, one of which is minimization of any unwanted reflected frequencies of light — including UV/fluorescent. Wearers are taught how to correctly launder their uniforms to prevent compromising safety.

Hunters have known this for years. There are several preparations sold to minimize UV signatures on their clothing. Myself, I have always stuck to natural fiber hunting clothing (no wash wool), and have been lucky enough to be able to hunt safely in areas that do not require Blaze Orange.

Pet food is loaded with UV dyes; check them out yourselves. Pet messes light up under UV light very nicely.

And… be aware of the newly approved, human “food grade” fluorescent dyes that are becoming increasingly common in the US food stream. NO GOOD!

Get yourself a “black light” flash and go have some fun. Fluorescence is all around us. 😉
