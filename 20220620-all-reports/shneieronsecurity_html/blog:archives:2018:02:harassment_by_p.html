Harassment By Package Delivery

People harassing women by delivering anonymous packages purchased from Amazon.

On the one hand, there is nothing new here. This could have happened decades ago, pre-Internet. But the Internet makes this easier, and the article points out that using prepaid gift cards makes this anonymous. I am curious how much these differences make a difference in kind, and what can be done about it.

This potentially could identify anyone physically stalking at the home or apartment, as well as providing evidence towards a court order. It’s likely that suspicious activity could be detected using open source software to analyze the video, even in real-time, but the most effective use would be tapping databases, both for face recognition and for pattern detection.

Depending on how much privacy the person being harassed is willing to trade, they could let Amazon or Nest analyze those feeds in real time. It’s a tricky problem for the Five Horsemen of the Tech Apocalypse to advertise that they are analyzing your video feeds using state-of-the-art “AI” software “to keep you safe.” The spooks pay extra for the feeds from banks and pharmacies. Even acoustic analysis of the interior of a house (SimpliSafe?) can be very effective. “To keep you safe,” is a euphemism for “learn everything that there is to know about you, so that we and our partners can exploit any defect in your attack surface (cognitive defenses) to sell you toxic plastic junk, poisonous food and beverages, planet-destroying vehicles, and take other any actions against your interests as we see fit.”

The astute will recall that H&R Block had a little problem with fraudulent tax returns being filed through their system. Management policy was to delay implementing reforms, because the fraudulent accounts generated wildly greater revenue than the legitimate ones. “Make me chaste, just not yet.” I would not wait for Amazon to police fraud of any kind on their site as long as it is making them money. The fake feedbacks are good for increasing sales and profits. When it becomes a drag on their business, e.g., as a result of successful lawsuits when they have damaged someone via inaction, they will be quick to act. Government will not be quick to act against any of the Five Horsemen, as they pay their protection monies timely. Any anti-trust actions can be interpreted as ratcheting up the percentage that they pay out for protection.
