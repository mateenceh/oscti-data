Jaron Lanier’s Ten Arguments for Deleting Your Social Media is quite persuasive. Newspapers are better than news feeds, and social interactions with friends are better when unmediated and unmanipulated by third parties. But I think he misses something.

Part of the appeal of Web 1.0 were the social encounters with people very different from yourself—serendipitous, often trivial but not infrequently substantial and enduring. I’m thinking of IRC, bulletin boards, early instant messaging services with randomization features. It seems that most of this has shifted to social media, except not really—the nature of the interactions are generally more superficial, fleeting and largely focused on metrics of popularity.

Have you noticed this and if not—assuming you reject social media, as Lanier does—how and where so you socialize online?
